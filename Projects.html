SeaPerch

I had built and designed a SeaPerch robot for Team Illusion Robots. Sea Perch is a competition where students build submarines using PVC pipes. The competition shows the students' creativity, understanding of concepts, and result of their time, effort, and dedication. When I built my Sea Perch Robot, I had to consider numerous things like the boundancy of the ROV, the speed, the mass, and even how much holes to drill into the robot. When building my robot, I learned about boundancy and hydrodynamics. Using those two concepts, I decided to make my seaperch robot. I decided to use CAD to first design my robot online and after that, I started building it. My design changed a little, but in the end, it still looks virtually the same, and after that, I started building the robot. Since this was my first time making a SeaPerch robot, I struggled with things like cutting the PVC pipes and even putting the motors in the right place. I needed help from my robotics staff, and thanks to their help, my robot was completed.



ESP32 Controlled Robotic Hand

One of my favorite projects that I worked on was a project where we put a robotic arm on top a wooden board with 4 wheels. Both the wheels and the robotic hand each were controlled by an ESP32. For the wheels, the PS3 controller connected to the ESP32, which enabled us to controlled the wheels and drive the vehicle using the controller. My job was to design and build the bottom portion of the project. That involved the wheels, PS3 controller, an ESP32, and 2 motor controllers. The project was very beneficial for me because not only didn't I get hands on experience, but I also learned how to code in the Arduino Cloud and C++, how to use an ESP32 and motor drives, and even how to plan a project. I am very happy I got to take a huge part in that project.


First Robotics Competition Robot

Another Project I worked on was the First Robotics Competition Robot(FRC). The First Robotics Competition is a tournament in which teams of high school students are required to build robots that fulfill the task required by the competition's instructors. This years theme for FRC was Reefscape. The objective of reefscape was to grab coral(16 inch diameter ball) and algae(21 inch PVC pipe) and to sort them and to put them where they belong. Building the FRC Robot was challenging and fun at the same time. I was responsible connecting the electronics and building the bumpers of the robot. Building the bumers and the electronics not only taught me how important teamwork and organization is but also taught me numerous parts of the FRC Robot.



Arduino Distance Sensor

One of the earliest projects I completed was the distance sensor project. This was when I was first starting using Arduino motherboard and the Arduino IDE. In this project, I coded the arduino to send an ultrasonic wave and to receive it. After that, I used a formula to calculate the distance by using the speed of the wave and how long it takes to come back to the distance sensor. This was the perfect project for me at the time to learn how to code in C++, how to use the Arduino IDE, and to learn about Pulse Width Modulation, and the pins on the arduino motherboard.
