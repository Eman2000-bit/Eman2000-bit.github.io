How I Got My Passion for Electrical Engineering

Growing up I always wondered what I was going to do in life. I always was attracted to creativity and the different designs of all kinds of things like origami, paper airplanes, super heroes and more, but as I grew up, I didn't know what career I wanted in the future. This changed when I was in 7th grade. During this time I was taught HTML and CSS. At first, the class was very difficult, and I thought that coding wasn't something I wanted to get involved in, but as time went on and as I successfully completed more tasks with html and css, I started to enjoy coding, and I decided that I wanted coding to be part of my future job.

When I reached high school, I started to grow a new interest in the other applications of electrical engineering. The truth is that I was just excited and just drawn into it. First it started out as something small like admiring cool inventions or watching people code on tiktok or youtube but soon it grew into something bigger. It grew into the desire to take action and to practice electrical engineering.

After this, I joined my school's Biomedical Engineering Club and later, Team Illusion Robotics. Due to these clubs, I got to work on different projects and learned numerous concepts, principles, and hardware.
