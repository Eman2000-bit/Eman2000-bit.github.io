Science Olympiad

2024-Now

The first year I participate in Science Olympiad was in the 2024-2025 school year. My activities were CodeBusters, Entomology, and Engineering CAD. Code Busters is cryptology activity, in which we try to break as much encoded information as possible. Entomology is an activity where we study insects. Finally, Engineering CAD is an activity in which we make 3d designs on Onshape in response to the blueprint that we are given. Since it was my first year, I didn't have any experience, but I didn't let that stop me. I worked hard, I learned, and I improved. I am glad I did these activities. Not only did they help me learn how to balance my time even more and teach me new skills, but they also challenged me. Engineering CAD taught me how to use Onshape, a Computer Aided Design(CAD) platform, which I am sure will be helpful in my future endeavors, Entomology help me learn more about insects, and make me curious about the different variations and types of insects, and codebusters taught me even more that perseverance is worth it, and in addition, it was the most mentally challenging thing I have done.




Peer Leading

2023-Now

I became a Peer Leader in the beginning of my 10th grade year. Peer Leading is exactly what it sounds like. It is being an example and a helper to my Peers in the class. My school has what is called a College Summit class, where all of us in the class work on assignments and important materials that is required for College Applications. This includes research, essays, FAFSA, and more. When there was problem or questions about an assignment, the students went to me. When the students didn't know what the assignment of the day was, they went to me, and even when the teacher was confused, he went to me. Being a Peer Leader was a way to gain experience leading and help both I and my peers prepare for college.

Refereeing

2022-Now

In 2022, I became both a member of the United States Soccer Federation(USSF) and the Capital Area Soccer REferees Assocation(CASRA). For clarification, you must become a certified and licensed USSF referee before you can join any other referee associations like CASRA. My USSF badge is my license and CASRA is the group of referees, assignors, and other officials I work with. There was a lot I learned when I was a referee. I learned that you need to ask the assignor to give you games. I learned how to address people, I learned how to keep the field of play safe, and I even learned soccer laws that I didn't even know as a soccer player. There are tough times as a referee like when I am refereeing through intense weather, or when both the parents and coach disagree with you and your decision, but overall, it is a fun and beneficial experience that I plan to continue doing.
